# Reminders

Ten non-negotiable principles. Load when a quick decision-rule check is needed,
or when the investor appears to be making a classic mistake.

---

1. **Never separate price from value.**
   A great company at a bad price is a bad investment.
   There is no such thing as a good or bad investment regardless of price.

2. **Always ask the second-level question.**
   What does everyone else think? How does my view differ?
   If my view matches consensus, I have no edge — the return is already priced in.

3. **Risk is permanent loss, not volatility.**
   Short-term price swings in a fundamentally sound investment are noise.
   The question is always: what is the probability of losing money permanently?

4. **Cycles always prevail.**
   Every trend eventually reverses. Trees don't grow to the sky.
   The most dangerous words: "this time it's different."

5. **Discomfort is often a signal.**
   If a contrarian position feels lonely and uncomfortable, it is more likely to be right.
   If buying feels comfortable and consensus, the upside is likely already priced in.

6. **Timing is secondary to price.**
   Buy at a large enough discount to value and you don't need to time perfectly.
   "Well bought is half sold."

7. **Margin of safety is non-negotiable.**
   Always assume your estimate of value could be wrong.
   Buy cheap enough that even a wrong estimate doesn't cause permanent loss.

8. **The risk paradox.**
   When everyone thinks it's risky, prices fall → it becomes less risky.
   When everyone thinks it's safe, prices rise → it becomes more risky.
   Fear and safety are inversely correlated with price.

9. **Patient opportunism beats forced action.**
   If nothing offers adequate margin of safety, cash is a valid position.
   The market does not reward activity; it rewards correct judgment.

10. **Be afraid when others are greedy; be greedy when others are afraid.**
    The pendulum swings between extremes. The investor's job is to exploit those
    extremes, not to be carried by them.

---

## Classic investor mistakes to flag

When you see these patterns in an investor's question, name them explicitly:

- **"It's gone up a lot — should I buy?"** → Recency bias + momentum chasing.
  Price appreciation is not evidence of investment merit.

- **"Everyone says this is the trade of the decade."** → Peak popularity risk.
  When all favorable facts are priced in, no new buyers remain.

- **"I can't afford to miss this."** → FOMO. The only penalty in investing is
  for making losing investments, not for missing winners.

- **"It's down 50% — it must be cheap."** → Price decline ≠ value.
  Ask whether intrinsic value has also declined, and by how much.

- **"The fundamentals don't matter right now."** → Momentum psychology.
  Fundamentals always matter eventually; the only question is timing.

- **"This is different from past bubbles."** → The most dangerous phrase.
  Every bubble feels different from the inside.

- **"I'll sell before it turns."** → Overconfidence in timing ability.
  By the time the reversal is obvious, the price has already moved.
